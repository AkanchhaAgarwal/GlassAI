The problem

Facade cleaning at height is one of the most dangerous routine jobs in the world.

Every glass tower is cleaned the same way it was decades ago: workers suspended on ropes and cradles, hundreds of metres above the ground, in heat and wind. The Gulf has some of the world's densest concentrations of high-rise glass — and the same manual method on every one of them.

Rope access

Workers descend tower facades manually on ropes and suspended cradles — exposed to falls, equipment failure, heat stress, and high winds.

Every tower

Glass facades need cleaning multiple times per year. In dusty Gulf climates, the frequency — and therefore the exposure — is even higher.

Zero automation

Despite decades of robotics progress, high-rise facade cleaning remains almost entirely manual. This is the gap GlassAI exists to close.

The solution

RopeBot — an AI suction robot that cleans glass the way a professional would.

RopeBot grips the facade on a minimum six-cup suction configuration, powered and secured through its own steel-core composite cable. It cleans in a three-step autonomous sequence, and its onboard AI vision verifies every pane before moving on.

Water mist spray

A fine mist loosens dust and particulate buildup — critical in desert climates — using a fraction of the water of manual washing.

Detergent application

Targeted detergent is applied only where the AI detects residue, keeping chemical use minimal and runoff controlled.

Squeegee wipe + AI verification

A motorised squeegee finishes the pane. Onboard vision inspects the result — streaks or missed spots trigger an automatic re-pass.

Deployment

Four anchoring systems. Almost any tower.

Buildings differ — so RopeBot's anchoring is modular. Four planned configurations cover the vast majority of high-rise facade types in the Gulf.

Config A

BMU-mounted deployment

Attaches to the Building Maintenance Units already installed on most modern Gulf towers — the fastest route to deployment with no structural changes.

Config B

Anchor bolt & pulley retrofit

For older towers without BMUs, a lightweight anchor-and-pulley system installed at roof level enables the same tethered descent.

Config C

Window-frame aluminium track

For curved and irregular facades, a slim track system follows the window frames, guiding RopeBot along non-vertical geometry.

Config D

Magnetic rail system

On steel-frame industrial and warehouse buildings, magnetic rails give RopeBot a secure path without drilling or permanent fixtures.

Safety by design

Fail-safe first. Everything else second.

A machine on a tower facade must be safer than the workers it replaces — by design, not by procedure. These principles are non-negotiable in RopeBot's engineering spec.

Passive mechanical lockingOn any power failure, mechanical locks engage automatically — the robot holds position with zero electrical input.
Supercapacitor backupOnboard supercapacitors provide reserve power to complete a safe-hold or controlled park if the tether supply is interrupted.
Wind-triggered emergency parkingLive wind sensing moves RopeBot to a secured parking position the moment gusts exceed safe thresholds.
Six-cup minimum suctionRopeBot never operates on fewer than six active suction cups — grip redundancy well beyond the load requirement.
Steel-core composite tetherThe power cable doubles as a rated structural tether — power delivery and fall restraint in a single line.
Live operator dashboardEvery deployment is monitored from the ground in real time — position, suction status, wind, and cleaning progress per pane.
